What Google Looks At

The Local Ranking Factors That Matter

Google's local algorithm weighs three core signals when deciding who shows up first. We optimize for all three.

1

Relevance

How well your profile information, categories, and description match what the searcher is looking for.

2

Distance

How close your business is to the searcher or the location they've specified in their search.

3

Prominence

How well-known and trusted your business is — driven by reviews, citations, and overall online presence.

Local SEO FAQ

Google Business Profile FAQs

Answers to the most common questions about Google Business Profile and local search visibility — and why it matters for every local business.

Google Business Profile (GBP) is your free listing on Google Search and Maps. When someone searches 'digital marketing agency near me' or 'best agency in Chennai', the businesses that appear in the top 3 map results get 70%+ of all local clicks. Without an optimised GBP, your competitors take those leads.
An unclaimed or unoptimised listing is worse than none — it may show wrong hours, missing photos, no reviews, and outdated info. Optimised profiles get 5× more profile views and 7× more direction requests than incomplete ones. Claiming and optimising properly is what drives real local traffic.
The Local Pack is the map block with 3 business listings that appears at the top of Google for local searches. Getting into it requires strong GBP signals (reviews, categories, NAP consistency), local SEO on your website, and authority signals. Growfic specialises in Local Pack optimisation.
Extremely. Reviews impact both ranking (more reviews = stronger local signal) and conversions (buyers trust businesses with 4.5+ star ratings). A business with 10 reviews loses customers to a competitor with 100. We help you build a consistent, authentic review generation system.
Negative reviews aren't necessarily damaging if handled correctly. A professional, empathetic response to a negative review builds more trust than ignoring it. We help you craft responses and, where Google policies allow, flag fake or spam reviews for removal.
